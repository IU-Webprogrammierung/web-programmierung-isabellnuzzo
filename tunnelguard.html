TunnelGuard is my latest project of 2025, designed to revolutionize tunnel monitoring and management systems with a user-centric, integrated approach. The software addresses critical shortcomings in existing systems, such as fragmented interfaces, lack of integration, outdated technology, and inefficient workflows, which hinder operators' ability to make quick and informed decisions during emergencies.

Project Psychology

Problem

Current tunnel monitoring systems are fragmented, outdated, and inefficient, posing significant challenges to operators during emergencies and day-to-day operations. These systems lack centralized integration, forcing operators to switch between multiple interfaces, which delays critical decision-making. Inadequate visualization of real-time data, limited automation, and outdated hardware further contribute to inefficiencies.

This disjointed setup results in increased error potential, slow response times, and challenges in coordinating actions during critical situations like fires, floods, or system malfunctions. Additionally, insufficient security measures and a lack of scalability hinder the ability to adapt to modern technologies and future infrastructure needs.

Solution

TunnelGuard offers a modern, centralized, and user-focused solution to revolutionize tunnel monitoring and management. The system introduces a revolutionary three-screen setup that ensures no critical data is ever out of the operator's field of vision. Each screen is dedicated to a specific function: the main dashboard for centralized controls, live video feeds for real-time monitoring, and a third screen for protocols and detailed data analysis. This design allows operators to access all necessary information simultaneously, eliminating the need for constant tab-switching and ensuring faster, more informed decision-making during emergencies and routine operations.

Key features include real-time anomaly detection with visual alerts, automated emergency protocols, and flexible customization options to prioritize critical tasks. Advanced security measures protect against cyber threats, while the streamlined interface supports seamless navigation and improved usability.

Login Screen

Before

The Login Screen for TunnelGuard has been designed to provide operators with a clear, secure, and intuitive access point to the system. The interface is divided into two sections: a visual placeholder on the left, allowing for branding or relevant imagery, and the functional login form on the right, ensuring a clean and focused layout.

Key elements include fields for email and password, accompanied by options to remember login credentials and recover forgotten passwords, enhancing user convenience. A prominent "Sign in" button facilitates access, while a link below the form allows new users to sign up seamlessly. The minimalist design ensures a distraction-free experience, tailored for operators who require efficiency and simplicity.

Dashboard

Before

The Dashboard Screen of TunnelGuard serves as the central hub for operators, providing a real-time overview of critical system data and controls. Designed for efficiency and clarity, the dashboard is divided into intuitive sections, enabling operators to monitor and manage multiple aspects of tunnel operations seamlessly.

On the left, a camera grid displays live video feeds from multiple tunnel cameras, with indicators showing the status and resolution of each feed. Operators can add or configure cameras and toggle between automatic and manual modes for greater flexibility.

The center panel features a task manager integrated with a calendar, allowing operators to track and prioritize urgent tasks, such as system monitoring and protocol reviews. Tasks are dynamically highlighted by urgency to ensure immediate attention.

On the right, a detailed metrics panel provides key environmental data, including light intensity, smoke levels, CO2 measurements, and water levels. Operators can adjust settings like ventilation or water pumps through intuitive toggle switches, ensuring quick and effective responses to changing conditions.

Analytics and Traffic

Before

The Analytics & Traffic Screen of TunnelGuard provides a comprehensive overview of traffic flow and environmental conditions within and around the tunnel system. This screen is designed to enable operators to monitor real-time data, anticipate potential disruptions, and make informed decisions to ensure smooth traffic management and safety.

The left panel features a traffic flow graph that combines vehicle volume and warning notifications over time, allowing operators to identify trends and potential bottlenecks. Below, a detailed weather forecast and its expected impact are displayed, showing metrics like humidity, precipitation, wind direction, and an energy balance graph for operational efficiency.

The right panel focuses on real-time traffic updates, displaying current queue lengths and waiting times at tunnel portals. Alerts for scheduled closures or disruptions are clearly highlighted, ensuring proactive planning. Detailed graphs for the north and south portals visualize traffic conditions, and notifications of ongoing construction projects provide essential context for operational adjustments.

Protocols

Before

The Protocols Screen of TunnelGuard provides operators with a centralized platform to manage and execute emergency and operational protocols efficiently. This screen is designed to ensure quick responses to critical incidents while maintaining a clear overview of ongoing tunnel activities.

The left panel highlights security alerts, listing actionable issues such as increased water levels, camera maintenance requirements, blocked emergency exits, and SOS notifications. Each alert includes a "show" option, enabling operators to access detailed information and take immediate action.

Below the alerts, a comprehensive list of predefined protocols is displayed, covering scenarios like fires, flooding, hazardous materials, power outages, traffic accidents, and evacuations. Operators can view, edit, or execute these protocols directly from the interface. Additionally, the system allows for the creation of new protocols, ensuring flexibility and adaptability for evolving needs.

On the right, a live tunnel map provides a visual representation of the tunnel, showcasing the locations of phones, cameras, and other critical infrastructure. This ensures that operators can monitor and respond to incidents with precision.
