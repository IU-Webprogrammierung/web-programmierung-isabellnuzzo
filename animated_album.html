Kiki’s Delivery Service

Animated Album Cover

Visual Tribute to a Timeless Coming-of-Age Soundtrack

This animated album cover is a personal homage to Kiki’s Delivery Service – Vocal Album (Yumi Arai / Joe Hisaishi, Studio Ghibli, 1989). Inspired by the album’s themes of independence, change, and growth, the scene uses hand-drawn-style animation and a pastel color palette to convey emotion and movement without words.

At the center: a faceless scarecrow in an endless spring landscape, surrounded by swaying grass, surreal clouds, and a crow in flight. Every element loops seamlessly to reflect the idea of cyclical growth, leaving comfort, facing challenge, and returning transformed.

Everything is hand-drawn in ProCreate and the animation was created entirely in Adobe After Effects. This piece challenged me to combine illustration, animation, and storytelling into a cohesive loop. It demonstrates my ability to develop concepts, work symbolically, and deliver polished motion design, even with no prior experience in the software.

Roles & Tools

  • Roles: Concept · Illustration · Motion Design · Compositing
  • Tools: Adobe After Effects · Photoshop · Figma
  • Duration: 12 weeks

From Sketch to Screen – Visual Development

Every creative journey starts with a rough idea. This storyboard was my first step in translating a feeling into motion; a hand-drawn prototype that tested composition, rhythm, and narrative clarity before any animation began.

These early frames explored how visual elements like the scarecrow, crow, and looping leaf might interact. I used this sequence to test out core timing, balance, and movement. Even at this early stage, I was thinking in loops: about cyclical growth, return, and subtle transformation.

What began as a static sketch soon evolved into a seamless animation with motion-tuned layers, atmospheric lighting, and refined character behaviors. Through iterative refinement and visual testing, I turned this static idea into a looping motion piece that now lives and breathes on screen.
